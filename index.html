Introduction

Goal: Exploration of efficiency trends in the car industry

1. Show by car make, compare two car makes at a time
2. Allow adjustment of the span of years
3. Include an overlay of historical oil prices

Data: Fuel economy dataset retreived from fueleconomy.gov

- Filtered and prepped for the app
- Created a measure “M22”: Yearly % of models with MPG > 22
- M22 measure based on non-standardized counts reported by company
- Oil price dataset scraped from inflationdata.com, price adjusted to 2017 dollars - Merged in the oil data to enable a graphical overlay

Code that creates the dataset

cars <- read.csv("cars.csv")
oilP <- read.csv("oil prices.csv", header = FALSE, col.names = c("year", "pr", "adj"))
makes <- sort(unique(cars$make))
cardat <- cars %>% 
    filter(year < 2017 & (fuel == "Regular" | fuel == "Premium")) %>%
    select(year, make, disp, mpg, class) %>%
    group_by(make, year) %>% 
    summarize(m22 = round(100 * mean(mpg >= 22), 1),
              mean_disp = round(mean(disp), 1)) %>%
    mutate(oil = oilP[match(year, oilP$year), "adj"], 
           yr = str_pad(year %% 100, 2, pad = "0"))
write.csv(cardat, "cardat.csv")

Shiny App Instructions

  • The plot shows car make efficiency: number of cars with MPG > 22 each year
  • The plot points are shaded when the global price of oil exceeded $50 per barrel
  • Use pull-down lists to choose another car maker
  • Type or click to alter the range of years, from 1984 through 2016
  • There are no input controls designed into the UI yet (e.g. years prior to 1984)
